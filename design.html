Our Architecture

The design of "Going Home" is dependent on a number of key components: the player, enemies, menu system, puzzles, and traps. Below entails each of these components and their corresponding attributes. Each component has an associated script with code to program these attributes. Each component also has an associated scene. The organization in the Godot engine is as follows: a folder for scenes and a folder for scripts. Both folders have similar subfolders that comprise each of the key components. All work passed off to each team member must be an independent part of the game from the rest. This means that work can be done independently without interferring the work of others. This streamlines the development process and is more efficient.

The architecture diagram above gives an overall visual of all the components and how they are connected as part of the design.

Menus

    -Main Menu

      -Start button: Launches game into first level scene

      -Options button: Opens up a new scene containing audio and graphics settings buttons

      -Quit game button: Throws an exit command and quits back to OS

    -Pause menu

      -Resume button: Resumes game and unpauses it

      -Quit button: Exits game to main menu scene

    -Player Dead Menu

      -Restart button: Reloads the current level from beginning

      -Return button: Returns to main menu scene



Player

    -Movement: 

      -check for walking left or right

      -check for sprinting

      -check for dash

      -check for direction of player

      -check for respawn of player

    -Cooldowns: for dash

    -Status checks:

      -health of player

      -can double jump

 

Enemies

    -Ranged Type:

      -Aimless shooting: Enemy entities that stay in a fixed position and are constantly launching hitboxes that hurt the player

      -Shooting at player: Enemy entities that only shoot when the player is in detection range. Projectile hitboxes are launched directly at the player until the player leaves detection range

 

    -Melee Type:

      -Aimless wandering: Enemy entities that move from left to right and cause damage when in contact with the player

      -Follows player: Enemy entities that move from left to right and cause damage when in contact with the player. If the player is in detection range, it will follow it until the player leaves detection range

 

Traps

  -Pressure plate switches

  -Spawn enemies: Buttons on the ground that, when stepped on by the player, will spawn hostile entities

  -Shoot projectiles: Buttons on the ground that, when stepped on by the player, will launch hitboxes that will hurt the player

 -Spike trap: Immediately kills player when they collide

 -Water trap: Immediately kills player when they collide



Puzzles

    -Pressure plate switches

      -Open doors: Buttons on the ground that, when stepped on by the player, will open up doors to access other areas
